# NO BULLSHIT GRIEF

## Where grief gets loud, truth gets ugly, and survival gets a goddamn attitude.

Welcome to the code cave behind **No Bullshit Grief** — the digital home of grief without glitter, platitudes, fake healing timelines, or emotionally constipated “stay strong” nonsense.

This project powers the online presence for **nobullshitgrief.com**, a raw, darkly funny, brutally honest grief platform built for the people who are tired of being handed inspirational refrigerator magnets while their entire life is on fire.

This isn’t a soft little wellness site.
This is grief with teeth.

---

## What This Site Is

**No Bullshit Grief** is a survival hub for people living through loss, trauma, emotional wreckage, and the weird hellscape that comes after someone important dies and the world has the audacity to keep spinning.

It exists for the people who:

- hate empty comfort phrases
- laugh at wildly inappropriate times because the alternative is screaming
- are tired of grief being sanitized for other people’s comfort
- need honesty more than they need another candle graphic
- want language that actually sounds like the inside of a shattered human brain

This site is part blog, part brand hub, part emotional weapon system.

---

## What Lives Here

This repository contains the working guts of the **No Bullshit Grief** web presence, including site structure, content layouts, styling, assets, and whatever other beautiful chaos gets duct-taped into existence along the way.

Expected sections may include:

- **The Aftermath Diaries** — grief blog posts with bite marks
- **Survival Shit** — products, tools, downloads, and resources
- **About** — the human behind the smoke, ash, and profanity
- **Book/Author Pages** — home base for grief books and related projects
- **Legalish Shit** — policies, disclaimers, and other necessary buzzkill documents
- **Contact / Holler At Me** — because apparently websites need doors

## Brand Vibe

The aesthetic is:

- black-on-black
- blood red
- ash gray
- smoke, grit, texture, scars
- typewriter energy
- biker-jacket grief counselor
- beautiful, brutal, and allergic to bullshit

The tone is:

- raw
- darkly funny
- profanity-friendly
- honest as hell
- compassionate without being cutesy
- sharp enough to cut through the grief fog

No beige healing circles.
No “everything happens for a reason.”
No emotional bubble wrap.

---

## Core Message

Grief doesn’t need to be pretty to be valid.
Survival doesn’t have to look graceful.

And healing?

Healing can show up late, drunk, furious, barefoot, and still count.

This site is for the people still standing in the aftermath, even if they’re standing there with bloodshot eyes, bad jokes, and one functioning nerve left.
